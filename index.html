Company Overview
  • Name: Slice Beer Co. (Slice Beer Company)
  • Tagline: "Culture of Fresh"
  • Founded: November 2019, in Lincoln, CA (Sierra Foothills, Placer County)
  • Founders: Zach Frasher (brewer; previously Moonraker Brewing, Auburn) and Russ Yeager (also marketing director for Old Town Pizza next door)
  • Address: 665 6th Street, Lincoln, CA 95648
  • Phone: (916) 408-6889
  • Email: beer@slicebeer.com
  • Website: slicebeer.com · Online ordering: order.slicebeer.com (Square)
  • Hours: Mon to Thu 3PM to 10PM · Fri to Sat 11AM to 10PM · Sun 11AM to 9PM
  • Social: Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter @slicebeer
  • Reviews: Untappd, Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp
  • Production:* 10-barrel brewhouse, 120-barrel fermentation capacity, ~1,500 barrels per year; taproom seats ~32. Taproom only, no outside distribution.
Key Products / Services
  • Fresh, rotating beer: Hop-forward West Coast IPAs, juicy hazy IPAs, plus classic styles. Heavy tap rotation is the core promise.
  • Flagship beer: "Original Juice," a hazy IPA.*
  • On Tap (Untappd): Live tap list so customers see what is pouring before they visit.
  • Taproom: Walk-in taproom in Old Town Lincoln; outdoor and group-friendly seating; Old Town Pizza next door for food.
  • Online shop (Square): Cans, merch, and gift cards for taproom pickup.
  • Catering & events (NEW): Keg delivery, taproom buyouts, and private/event beer across Lincoln, Roseville, Rocklin, and greater Sacramento.* Packages, radius, and pricing to be confirmed.
Key Differentiators
  • Fresh over everything: small, heavily rotated batches. Being small is the purpose, not a limitation.
  • Award pedigree: Frasher's triple IPA bested Russian River's Pliny the Younger at a 2017 competition; in 2020 Slice was the first brewery to win people's choice for both the double and triple IPA at the Bistro's DIPA/TIPA Festival.*
  • On-site hop farm: brewery, hop farm, and tasting room together in the Sierra Foothills.
  • Pizza next door: Old Town Pizza adjacency gives a built-in food and "pizza and beer" draw competitors lack.
  • Influences: New England pioneers Hill Farmstead and Tired Hands inform the hazy program.
Competitive Landscape
  • In Lincoln: Goathouse Brewing, Dueling Dogs Brewing.
  • Rocklin / Roseville / Loomis: Moksa Brewing (Rocklin, IPA-led), Out of Bounds Brewing (Roseville), Final Gravity (Roseville bottle shop/taps), Loomis Basin Brewing, Granite Bay Brewing.
  • Auburn: Moonraker Brewing, Crooked Lane Brewing, Alehouse Annex.
  • Positioning: Slice wins on freshness, rotation, IPA pedigree, and the Old Town Pizza pairing. Geo strategy is to convert Roseville, Rocklin, and Sacramento searchers for the short drive north into Lincoln.
  • Catering market: regional brewery catering and keg delivery is active (Urban Roots, Jackrabbit, Fieldwork, Midland). Slice's opening is fresh-beer keg delivery and buyouts centered on Placer County.
